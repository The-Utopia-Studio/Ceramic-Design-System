# Personas

## Summary

The Utopia Design System serves a venture studio environment, not only a conventional product team. Its users include founders, engineers, designers, AI agents, maintainers, Arabic/RTL reviewers, and product operators.

Each persona needs the system to reduce ambiguity, improve quality, and make early product work more reusable.

## Founder Or Venture Co-Build Partner

Founders may work with Utopia before a product exists. They need to move from idea to credible first product without waiting for every interface decision to be invented from scratch.

Needs:

- Fast product foundations for first builds.
- Credible templates for landing pages, apps, forms, dashboards, and AI surfaces.
- Market-ready patterns for Middle East, Southeast Asia, and Africa contexts.
- UI foundations that can mature into production instead of being discarded.

Success means a founder can reach a serious first product faster with less rework.

## Product Engineer

Product engineers build the actual interfaces. They need stable components, clear imports, predictable styling, and implementation rules that reduce local decisions.

Needs:

- Typed React components and stable entrypoints.
- Core CSS and theme CSS that can be installed in consumer apps.
- Semantic tokens instead of raw brand values.
- Radix/shadcn-founded primitives where appropriate.
- Examples that resemble production use cases.

Success means engineers can build faster while staying inside the system contract.

## Product Designer

Product designers need a shared language that maps design decisions to implementation behavior.

Needs:

- Named components and templates.
- Clear token roles.
- Core versus theme boundaries.
- Utopia Default visual policy.
- Arabic-optimized layout and typography rules.
- AI workflow constraints for generated explorations.

Success means design explorations translate cleanly into production UI.

## AI Coding Agent

AI agents need structured context before generating UI. They should not infer Utopia rules from screenshots or local pages.

Needs:

- Dense CLI output.
- Machine-readable manifests.
- Component contracts.
- Template skeletons.
- Theme policy.
- Arabic-optimized rules.
- Validation guidance.

Success means AI-generated UI uses approved components, tokens, templates, and theme rules without inventing local shortcuts.

## AI-Enabled Designer Or Developer

AI-enabled designers and developers may generate multiple product directions in parallel. They need the design system to keep those directions coherent enough to compare and review.

Needs:

- Agent-ready workflows.
- Clear review gates.
- Reusable templates.
- Rules for what AI must not invent.
- Fast validation of generated UI.

Success means AI increases exploration speed without reducing product quality.

## Design System Maintainer

Maintainers protect the integrity of the system.

Needs:

- Governance for new components, templates, and themes.
- Component audits.
- Token and manifest checks.
- Release discipline.
- Clear rules for what belongs in core, theme, or consumer apps.

Success means the system grows without becoming inconsistent.

## Arabic, RTL, And Market Readiness Reviewer

Arabic and RTL reviewers ensure the system serves Arabic-speaking users as baseline users.

Needs:

- RTL layout rules.
- Arabic typography rules.
- Bidirectional text support.
- Localization guidance for dates, numbers, currency, names, forms, search, validation, and error states.
- Accessibility checks in Arabic and RTL contexts.

Success means Arabic support is designed into the system rather than patched after implementation.

## Product Lead Or Operator

Product leads care about speed, quality, and repeatability across ventures.

Needs:

- Clear adoption reasons.
- Success metrics.
- Reliable AI quality-control workflows.
- Market-readiness confidence.
- A path from prototype to production.

Success means Utopia can build more products with less repeated setup cost and less design drift.
